Enemy table

Decode every enemy's stat record and 3D battle model straight from your own disc image. The full archive — names, HP, MP, combat stats, and the embedded mesh for the whole roster — is LZS-decompressed in your browser via WebAssembly. Click any row to spin its model. No game data ships with this site; bring your own .bin and nothing leaves your machine.

# Name HP MP ATK DEF↑ DEF↓ AGL SPD SP Magic Spells (SP) EXP Gold Drop 3D

All six stats are pinned from the battle code: FUN_80054CB0 copies each record halfword into the live actor, and the formula consumers name them — ATK (attacker offense), DEF↑/DEF↓ (the two defense facets, selected by attack move index), AGL (accuracy/evasion roll seed), SPD (turn-order initiative seed), and SP (the spirit/action gauge the AI spends selecting spells). EXP / Gold / Drop are the victory-spoils fields (record +0x44..+0x49); these are the base values - the retail spoils formula scales them (EXP ×3/4 split among the party; a lone enemy's gold yields floor((gold>>1)/2)). Drop shows #item-id @ chance%. Source layout: PROT entry 0867_battle_data, one fixed 0x14000-byte LZS slot per monster id. Details on the battle formulas page.

How it works

The stat archive is the extended footprint of PROT entry 867. Each monster id n (1-based) maps to slot (n−1)·0x14000, framed as [u32 decoded-size][Legaia LZS stream]; the decoded block's head is the stat record the battle loader copies into a live actor. The same legaia_asset::monster_archive parser used by the native tools and the engine runs here, compiled to WebAssembly.

Files are read with the browser's FileReader API directly into WebAssembly memory. Nothing is uploaded; the project ships zero Sony bytes (bring-your-own-disc). Native equivalent: asset monster-archive extracted/PROT/0867_battle_data.BIN (add --id N for a single record).

The 3D model

The same decoded block carries the enemy's battle model: a Legaia TMD embedded at the block-relative offset in the stat record's +0x04 field (right after the name string) — the same pointer the battle loader installs at actor +0x230. 186 of the 194 archive slots carry a clean TMD here (the rest are filler ids). The viewer renders it textured and directional-lit: the colours come from the texture / palette pool at record +0x08, decoded from the battle loader FUN_80055468 — a 0x1E0-byte region of fifteen 16-colour palettes followed by a 4bpp page (always 256 rows tall, 128 or 256 texels wide). Each primitive picks its palette from its CBA (cba & 0x3F) and samples the page at its UVs, exactly as the PSX GPU does from VRAM. Native export: asset monster-archive … --id N --obj enemy.obj --texture-png enemy.png.
