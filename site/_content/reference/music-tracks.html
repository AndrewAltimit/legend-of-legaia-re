Music-track disambiguation

Every in-game BGM cue cross-referenced across its four naming spaces: the internal debug sound-test ID + working title, the in-game context it actually plays in, the official OST album title, and a proposed relocalization title. The human-readable label layer for the SEQ/BGM tracks the preservation tools extract and the engine resolves.

Four names for one track

A single Legaia track has, in practice, four different names depending on where you read it, and they do not line up one-to-one:

  • Debug sound-test ID - the internal asset/track identifier the debug sound test exposes (M01, M14A, M26B1, plus a few non-M labels like ALNDRA, JBGM, KANASIMI). Closest handle to the on-disc track order.
  • Debug sound-test title - the short Japanese working title shown beside that ID; describes the scene the track was written for.
  • In-game context - where the track is actually heard (often broader than the debug title; tracks get reused across scenes).
  • Official OST title - the title the published soundtrack album gives the track, where one exists.

BGM lookup in retail is PROT-relative (there is no literal track-name table in the executable - see audio and SEQ), so a map between the debug IDs and where each cue plays is the ground-truth labelling that the curated-data tables provide for items and enemies.

Provenance + confidence

Compiled by Henrique Stanke Scandelari (Stann0x) and contributed to this project as a reference. The romaji, English glosses, and proposed relocalization titles are his analysis; the debug-mode IDs/titles and OST titles are factual short labels transcribed from the game's own debug sound test and the published soundtrack listing.

Treated here exactly like the other curated reference tables: only the factual labels are committed - track IDs, the short scene-name titles, and where each cue plays - as ground-truth labels for the binary SEQ/VAB records under reverse engineering. No Sony-owned bytes (sequence data, sample data, executable) are reproduced; the audio itself is decoded at runtime from the user's own disc.

Confidence is Inferred unless a track is matched to its OST entry, in which case the OST columns are Confirmed against the published listing. The last three rows (highlighted in the source as uncertain) are flagged inline.

Notable entries

  • Borrowed placeholder cues. ALNDRA (#72) is Alundra's Zazzan battle theme and JBGM (#73) is the Wild Arms battle theme - both other Contrail/Sony titles, present on the Legaia disc as dev placeholders.
  • Dev test files. DUMMY (#74, “I'm a Dummy-dayo!”), PIANO (#78), LEVELUP (#79), and A (#80) are test/placeholder entries, not shipping music.
  • Reused themes under different IDs. 中ボス / “medium boss” appears for M27, M108, M33; ディスコ / “disco” for M112, M120, M117 - the same idea staged for different scenes.
