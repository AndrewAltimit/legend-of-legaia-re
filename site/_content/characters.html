Field form vs battle form

The game keeps two versions of each hero. The field form is a small, low-poly model that stays loaded across every town and dungeon, so walking through a door never has to re-fetch the party. The battle form is a higher-detail model that a real battle builds on the fly: the engine reads each character's battle file from the disc and assembles the model piece by piece from whatever gear the character has equipped - body, weapon, and Ra-Seru pieces are each picked by the equipped item. The disc also ships a pre-assembled default-gear copy of the battle models (what the Baka Fighter minigame uses, and what this viewer shows), and the viewer overlays the true in-battle colours decoded from each character's battle file.

The one-byte equipment swap

On the field, equipment barely changes the model at all. Each hero's field model carries two spare, pre-baked variants of its weapon-holding part, and the game flips between them based on a single byte in the character's save record - equipped or not. That one bit is the entire field-side equipment mechanism; which specific weapon you carry is conveyed through the texture, not new geometry. The toggle in the viewer above performs exactly that swap.

asset character-pack … --slot 0 --equip 1 --out vahn_equipped.tmd

The animations are the real in-game clips

Every clip in the Animation panel comes off your disc: the walk and idle cycles the party plays on the field, the per-scene NPC clip pools, and the battle-stance clips. A character is stored as separate body pieces that the animation data assembles - each frame places every piece at its joint, exactly as the game's renderer does - so playback here matches what you see in-game, and frame 0 of an idle clip is the rest pose. The battle form is posed from the real battle animation data the same way.
