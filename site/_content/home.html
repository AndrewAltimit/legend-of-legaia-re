Legend of Legaia — reverse engineering & clean-room engine port

Two coordinated tracks under one repo. Track 1: extract every asset on the disc, document every format with provenance back to a Ghidra function, build round-trip parsers. Track 2: a clean-room Rust engine port that consumes the extracted data, in the same legal pattern as ScummVM, OpenRCT2, OpenMW, and OpenLara. Bring your own disc image; the toolkit handles the rest.

Where we are

The disc is fully readable. Every major subsystem is traced and documented. Three out of three script VMs are decoded; two are ported. The battle and combat-stat machinery is decoded. Asset preservation track ships round-trip parsers for every documented format.

Disc + asset preservation
Mode2/2352 reader, ISO9660 walker, PROT.DAT TOC math, 24 format detectors classifying ~80% of PROT entries by class. Round-trip parsers ship for TIM, TMD, VAB, MES (partial), ANM, MDT, LZS, scene bundles.
Static analysis
1310 function dumps committed; 98.5% closed-loop on the citation graph (1119 / 1136 cited helpers covered). Three game VMs decoded. All major SCUS subsystems traced.
Engine port — Phase 1
Asset viewer with PSX VRAM emulation, textured TMD rendering, stage geometry, VAB playback. In-browser version live.
Engine port — Phase 2
Runtime port. Actor + Field VMs fully ported (13 + 43 opcodes). Move VM port in progress (71 + 61 opcodes).
Engine port — Phase 3
Gameplay assembly. Field map + dialog, battle subsystem, menus, save/load. Not started.
Open work
Move-VM port wrap-up, dialog font glyph atlas extraction (need VRAM dump), MES dialog renderer (overlay-resident), DATA_FIELD per-field layout final reverse.
