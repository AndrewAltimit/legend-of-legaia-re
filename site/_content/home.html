Legend of Legaia reverse engineering & clean-room engine port

Legend of Legaia is a 1998 PlayStation RPG, and this project has taken it apart end to end: browse its assets, hear its music, watch its cutscenes, patch its disc with a randomizer, and run a clean-room engine - right in this browser, from your own disc image. Bring your own disc; this site and repo ship zero Sony bytes. The engine port follows the same legal pattern as ScummVM, OpenRCT2, OpenMW, and OpenLara.

Try it now

Everything below runs client-side in this browser. No install; pick a disc image once and it's cached locally for every page.

ROM patcher (randomizer)
Randomize your disc in the browser: drops, encounters, chests, shops, doors, arts combos, and code-injected extras like the in-shop Seru-trading vendor. Outputs a patched image; nothing is uploaded.
Asset viewer
Browse every 3D mesh and 2D texture on the disc, grouped by scene - plus a live engine panel that ticks the runtime VMs.
Media browser
Play every music track and sound bank, and watch every FMV cutscene with synced video and audio.
Enemy table
Every enemy's stats, drops, steals, and spells - click a row to spin its animated 3D battle model, or export it as glTF.
Characters
Vahn, Noa, and Gala in 3D - field and battle forms, equipment swaps, and real in-game animation playback.
NPCs
The whole cast of every town and dungeon - villagers, shopkeepers, story figures - posed and animated from your disc.
Game world
Every town and dungeon rendered in 3D on the page - pick a location from the navigator and its full map assembles, joined with enemy rosters, bosses, and shops.
World overview (3D)
Each kingdom's overworld assembled in real-time WebGL 3D - terrain, landmarks, ocean animation, fog.
Shops & vendors
Town → vendor → priced inventory → item detail, across every shop in the game.
Minigames & Sol Tower
Sol Tower floor map, slot prize ladders, Muscle Dome courses, Baka Fighter rounds, and side-quest chains.
Tactical Arts
Per-character art lists with direction-token inputs and AP costs - and with your own disc, watch each art performed by the real battle model, or download it with every battle animation as a .glb.
