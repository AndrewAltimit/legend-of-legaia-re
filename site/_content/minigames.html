Three fixed-progression arenas. Entry fee is a flat 100 coins. Restrictions are enforced at course entry - Beginner and Expert disable equipment and items but allow magic; Master adds a magic ban. Accessories are always permitted.

Internals (reverse-engineered)

The tables above are the player-facing prize / roster data. Each minigame's runtime is reverse-engineered too - its per-frame state machine, scoring math, and data tables, traced from the captured overlay code:

  • Fishing - a reel tug-of-war against a tension gauge, with the 10 fish species driven by a per-species parameter table decoded from the disc.
  • Slot machine - a reel state machine with two random-number generators (one for the reels, one for balance-bracketed feature odds) and a per-symbol payout table; your per-spin balance only commits to the shared casino coin bank when you cash out at the counter - which is why an "Infinite Coins" cheat reads full at the cage but doesn't make spins free.
  • Baka Fighter - rock-paper-scissors, best of 3, against 17 scripted opponents each with their own prize and attack pattern.
  • Dance - a beat clock with an accuracy-weighted timing judge, a baked step chart selected by difficulty, and per-tier score multipliers.
  • Muscle Dome - a card battle: deal a hand, commit cards into the fighter's action queue under a point budget, and resolve through the game's regular battle engine (it lives inside the battle code, not its own minigame module).

All five are playable in the clean-room engine: in the engine's play-window, dance (K), fishing (L), the slot machine (O), Baka Fighter (B), and the Muscle Dome (M) run as suspending scene modes driven by ported rules engines that decode their tables from your disc at runtime. In Baka Fighter you counter the opponent's scripted pattern in a best-of-3 duel (a match win banks the ladder opponent's gold prize); in the Muscle Dome you commit strike-command cards under a point budget - the cards' costs are the same per-weapon bytes that size the battle Arts gauge.
