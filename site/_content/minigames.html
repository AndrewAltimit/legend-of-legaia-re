Sol Fever Disco — Noa's dance

Hit the step on the beat — the notes are the game's own step chart, and every closed step chain fills the groove gauge, promoting you to a denser, higher-scoring lane. Triangle is the wildcard: three “groovy moves” a song, worth the big multiplier only when spent on a 4-beat combo slot, and it spins Noa for up to a second during which nothing you press is judged. The two dancers beside you are running the same chart for real: beat them.

The judge scores the raw chart cell, not the held-sequence symbol the display substitutes on every fourth beat — and each chart symbol is literally a pad-button mask (1 = Square, 2 = Circle, 3 = Triangle).

Triangle matches no chart cell: it spends a stock of three, scores small anywhere but big (plus a full groove-gauge step) on the 4-beat combo slot, and the spin it triggers suspends judging entirely. Full trace, addresses and scoring constants: docs/subsystems/minigame-dance.md.

Baka Fighter — the duel

Rock-paper-scissors with life meters, best of three: 2 beats 1, 3 beats 2, 1 beats 3, and the opponent throws from a scripted pattern on the disc, so it can be read. Each hit you take feeds the combo counter behind the next one, and a comeback critical can fire at a sliver of HP. The Special is unbeatable but slow: it wins the exchange outright, or the whole round if it charges to full.

Start a run and pick a fighter on the cabinet's own PLAYER SELECT screen (arrows or a click, Enter confirms). After every stage win the retail tally menu asks NEXT GAME or PAY OUT — fight on and the pot rides (lose and it's forfeit), pay out and it banks. Clear all fourteen rungs and the whole pot pays, 460 coins from rung one; "Start at" lets you drop in at any rung.

The duel is drawn with the cabinet's own assets, decoded from your disc as it runs: your fighter from the battle-form party pack (PROT 1204), the opponent from its per-rung pack (PROT 1206…1219), both posed from their real animation banks, plus the arena, the retail HUD/tally-menu widgets, the overlay's own sound cues and its boss-overture music. Full trace: docs/subsystems/minigame-baka-fighter.md.

Fitted rather than traced: the 3D camera, fighter spacing and menu layout (the duel's GTE matrices aren't in a save state), the tiled floor (the stage set carries no floor mesh), the HP-bar frame, and the animation slot order. Two rules are stated readings rather than overlay-pinned constants: the mid-run pot forfeit and the final rung's automatic payout. When the presentation assets don't decode, the duel falls back to the stat panel above and says so — no stand-in art is drawn.

Vidna casino — slot machine

Three coins a spin, all five paylines always live — three straight, two diagonal — and you stop each reel yourself. A hidden net-take counter brackets the odds: the more the machine has taken off you, the more generous it rolls, and a big bonus win knocks the counter back down. Three blue kicks earn 1 bonus round, three red punches earn 3: the reels rotate onto the numeral strip (1–10) and stopping all three pays their product, 1 to 1000 coins — nothing steers those reels but your timing.

Space is the whole machine. Press it to spin, then press it once more per reel to stop them left to right — three presses, three reels, which is exactly what the cabinet's three stop buttons do. A win banks itself, so there is nothing to collect; when the coins run out, a press racks a fresh machine. Clicking the machine counts as a press, for want of a spacebar.

Fishing — the reel tug-of-war

Lock the cast meter to hook a fish — the harder you cast, the rarer the species — then reel it in. Reeling raises the tension gauge; let the line rest and it bleeds off. Pin the tension at the top and the line snaps; work the fish long enough and you land it for points. The catch scoring, the tension divisors and the point cap are the game's own confirmed kernels (FUN_801d4004 / FUN_801d5298); every fish's value is read off your disc's PROT 0972 species table.

Space casts and locks; hold J (or the Reel button) to reel. Press Space while the meter sweeps to hook a fish, then hold Reel to bring it in without snapping the line. After it lands or snaps, Space casts again.

The species table is your disc's. Each of the ten fish carries a score value, a pull factor and a strike gate in the fishing overlay's rodata (PROT 0972), decoded here and listed below; the locked cast power picks which one bites.

Fitted rather than traced: the two-bar pond drawing, the cast-meter sweep rate, and the land / snap thresholds — the exact reel-button bits and win/lose gates are on the open list in docs/subsystems/minigame-fishing.md. The tension arithmetic and catch award themselves are the overlay's confirmed formulas.

Muscle Dome — card battle

A four-card hand under a point budget: commit the cards you can afford into your fighter's action queue, confirm, and watch the round play out. Each committed card debits your budget; the opponent fills its own queue greedily. The deal, the budget gate, the hp × 0x6c / max score readout and the win/lose bookkeeping are the arena's own logic (FUN_801d0748 / FUN_801d388c) resident in the battle overlay (PROT 0898) on your disc.

Slot machines

Two coin-economy prize tables sit behind the slot-machine pulls: the Vidna casino exchanges casino coins won at its slots for items, while the Sol Tower F4 Muscle Dome counter exchanges the same coin pool (also earned through Muscle Dome runs and Baka Fighter rounds) for a higher-value prize ladder. Each ladder is capped at the 100,000-coin Earth Egg.

The Sol rows below are technically the Muscle Dome prize-exchange counter on F4, not the slot machine itself - the slot machine only pays out coins. The same coin pool funds Muscle Dome entry fees, Baka Fighter games, and counter purchases.

Three fixed-progression arenas. Entry fee is a flat 100 coins. Restrictions are enforced at course entry - Beginner and Expert disable equipment and items but allow magic; Master adds a magic ban. Accessories are always permitted.

Internals (reverse-engineered)

Each minigame's runtime - per-frame state machine, scoring math, data tables - is traced from the captured overlay code and written up in the linked repo docs:

  • Fishing - a reel tug-of-war against a tension gauge, with per-species parameter tables and two per-venue prize counters decoded from the disc - including a hidden 50,000-point War God Icon at Vidna the walkthrough prize lists miss.
  • Slot machine - a reel state machine with two RNGs and a per-symbol payout table; the feature odds bracket on a hidden net-take counter, and the per-spin balance only commits to the shared coin bank at the cash-out counter.
  • Baka Fighter - rock-paper-scissors, best of 3, against 17 scripted opponents each with their own prize and attack pattern.
  • Dance - a beat clock with an accuracy-weighted timing judge, a baked step chart selected by difficulty, and per-tier score multipliers.
  • Muscle Dome - a card battle: commit cards into the fighter's action queue under a point budget, resolved through the game's regular battle engine.
