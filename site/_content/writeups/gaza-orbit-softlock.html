The endless camera orbit - anatomy of a retail softlock

Since 1998, players have reported Legend of Legaia battles that simply stop: mid-fight, usually against the Gaza rematch, the action never comes and the camera circles the party forever. It happens on every regional release and survived every emulator. This is the story of catching it live, tracing it to a dead animation nobody re-stages, reproducing it on demand - and fixing it with nine instructions that make the game repair itself. The fix ships in the browser ROM patcher.

TL;DR
A monster approaching an out-of-reach target waits in a range-check poll with no movement code and no timeout; its staged “Move” animation is what actually carries it forward. A summon's staging round-trip immediately before the melee kills that animation ~12 vsyncs in, nothing re-stages it, and the fight waits forever. The patch rewrites nine redundant words inside the poll so a dead animation bounces the state machine back to its own staging state - the boss just resumes walking. Enable it with the Approach-softlock fix toggle in the ROM patcher.

The symptom, and the red herring

The freeze looks like a camera bug: the fight stops responding and the view keeps orbiting. But the idle azimuth sweep is unconditional - the battle round driver (FUN_801D0748) steps the camera yaw every frame without ever consulting the action state machine. The orbit is what a Legaia battle looks like whenever anything stalls; it says nothing about what stalled. The real question is why the per-action state machine, FUN_801E295C in the battle-action overlay (PROT entry 898, load base 0x801CE818), stopped advancing.

Two live captures answered it. A poll-only probe - no breakpoints, so it runs at recompiler speed while a human plays and savestates - flagged any action state held for thousands of frames. Both catches parked in the same place: state 0x19, the attack approach.

Four ways out of an attack

Every physical attack enters approach setup, state 0x14. Its first move is the range check FUN_8004E2F0(attacker, target) - 0 means “within reach” (a size-scaled radius, ~416 units for a boss-sized actor; party members use the static table DAT_80078870 = {256, 384, 1024}). From there the arm routes four ways:

The decisive branch is the walk gate. Out of reach, a monster attacker needs its approach-transition clip - action tag 0x20 in the per-monster action table (record+0x4C, scanned by first byte via FUN_80050E2C):

801e3260  li  a1,0x20              ; approach-transition tag
801e3268  jal 0x80050e2c           ; scan the action table
801e327c  bne v0,0xff,0x801e32b4   ; found  -> 0x15 (walk chain)
801e329c  li  a1,0x1               ; NOT found: stage tag 1 (the Move loop)
801e32a4  jal 0x80050e2c
801e32ac  j   0x801e32c4           ; -> 0x19, the wait-in-range poll
801e32b0  _sb v0,0x1da(s3)         ;    (delay: staged clip -> actor+0x1DA)

A roster sweep over the monster archive (PROT 867) puts numbers on it: 180 of the game's 186 monsters have no tag 0x20 - bosses included - so the fallback lane is not an edge case, it is how nearly every monster melee approaches. (All 186 do carry the tag-1 “Move” locomotion clip - the float or walk cycle the enemy viewer shows.)

An approach the state machine doesn't perform

Here is the trap in the design. The walking states 0x15–0x18 contain the state machine's own stepping code - a monster in that chain is moved by the engine, guaranteed to arrive. The fallback state 0x19 contains none: it re-derives facing, re-polls the range check, and on failure bumps a stall counter (ctx+0x6D4 - whose only reader turns out to be an interference-roll modifier, not a timeout). All actual movement comes from the staged animation's playback: the tag-1 Move clip slides the actor along its facing at a measured ~19 units per vsync.

Normally that works fine. Position captures of ordinary play show every healthy boss melee starting out of reach (438–1,078 units against a ~416 reach - standard formation spacing is out of reach; the models are just so large it looks adjacent) and the Move clip walking the boss in over 28–67 vsyncs, every time. The design only fails if the animation stops and nothing notices. Nothing can notice: 0x19 stages the clip once, in 0x14, and never again.

The trigger, reproduced: a summon, then the melee

What kills the clip? The second live catch answered it, because by then the probe was logging the actor's animation pair - staged clip (+0x1DA) versus playing clip (+0x1D9) - every vsync. The frame-exact onset:

v26536  summon cinematic: boss staged off-arena to (0,-2048),
        his damage-reaction clip plays (pair 2/2), restored, pair -> 0/0
v26867  summon action ends
v26873  boss melee starts DIRECTLY next - 0x14, target ~1022 away -> fallback
v26876  0x19, pair 1/1: Move clip ENGAGED, sliding ~19 units/vsync
v26888  pair 0/0: clip DEAD after ~12 vsyncs / ~236 units
        frozen at ~786 (> 416 reach) -> polls forever  = the softlock

Summons relocate every combatant for the cinematic - the boss is parked ~2,500 units off-arena and put back - and his hit-reaction clip plays while he's staged. When his very next action is the melee, the freshly staged Move clip terminates almost immediately: the staging round-trip leaves the animation driver holding something stale (a frame cursor or clip-length latch - the one detail still open), so the new clip hits its “end” in a dozen frames. Any other action in between resets whatever went stale: the same session shows a summon → someone else's turn → melee running the same clip 64 vsyncs and 1,260 units to a clean hit. The sequence was reproduced on the first directed attempt - cast a summon, stall so the boss's melee comes next, park.

It also explains the folklore. The bug clusters on late-game bosses (walk-less monsters with contact attacks and long fights full of summons), afflicts every region (nothing here is localization-touched), and looks random (it needs the summon → melee adjacency and an out-of-reach target - common, but not every round).

The fix: teach the poll to re-stage

A first fix simply skipped the approach - one word retargeting the walk-gate fallback straight into the strike chain, “hit from where you stand.” It closed the softlock and verified cleanly, but the roster numbers above made it unacceptably blunt: out-of-reach is the norm, so it would have deleted every boss's approach walk, not just the broken one. The shipped fix is the faithful one: fix the actual defect - the dead clip that nothing re-stages.

There was no room for new code (every verified-dead byte run in the executable is already occupied by other patch features - and the one promising candidate turned out to be a runtime buffer that 55 savestates all showed live: “zero is not dead”). The answer was to find nine expendable words inside the poll itself. State 0x19 re-derives the actor's facing every frame - but the target never moves during an approach, the facing was just computed at staging, and the strike arm recomputes it again on entry. Nine words of dead weight, exactly where the guard needs to live:

The guard reads the staged-clip byte each poll. Alive: nothing happens - healthy fights are byte-identical (a redundant re-stage can't even occur, because the write never happens). Dead while the poll still fails: set the state byte back to 0x14. That's the whole fix, because retail already knows how to stage an approach - its own 0x14 arm re-runs next frame, recomputes the facing, re-checks the range, re-stages the Move clip, and the boss resumes walking. If the clip ever dies again, the guard bounces again: a stuttering but monotonic approach that always arrives. The same bounce protects a party attacker whose run clip dies, and the only unrescuable shape - a monster with no Move clip at all - is vacuous: the roster sweep found zero.

Verification is layered the way the project verifies everything. A disc-gated oracle proves the edit is surgical (exactly the nine words change, byte-deterministic, idempotent, refused on an unrecognized image). And the runtime proof is hands-off replays of both live-caught park savestates with only the nine words poked into RAM: in each, the guard bounces exactly once, retail re-stages (pair back to 1/1), the boss walks in at ~19 units/vsync, the strike lands, and the round completes.

Run it on your disc

The fix is the “Approach-softlock fix” toggle in the in-browser ROM patcher - your disc image never leaves the tab - or legaia-patcher randomize --input DISC.bin --approach-softlock-fix from the command-line patcher, which can also emit a PPF patch. It is seedless, composes with every other option, and the Balanced / Full Chaos presets enable it by default.
