Tactical Arts

Per-character art lists with direction-token inputs and AP costs. Each row is one of the four kinds the engine distinguishes - Regular Arts (taught from level 1, learned by use), Hyper Arts (chain-combos with longer inputs), Super Arts (unlocked via story / shrine triggers), and the single per-character Miracle Art. Data is driven by data/gamedata/arts.toml and cross-validated against the per-character art-name tables in crates/art.

Input legend

Yellow = trigger input (the finisher that lands the art)
Purple = setup input (queued combo step)

Direction is the raw D-pad byte the engine stores in the Art Record (1=L, 2=R, 3=D, 4=U). Token mapping per character is:

Watch the arts

Load your own disc image and every art card below becomes clickable: the canvas shows the character performing that art, exactly as a battle stages it. The model is assembled in your browser from the character's player battle file (the equipment-section splice the retail battle loader runs - character-mesh reference), textured from the same file's texture pools and decoded battle palette, and posed from the art's on-disc keyframe stream: the art-animation bank at record[0] +0x58, its clips resolved through the readef.DAT "ME" archives (side-band reference). Between arts the character holds the record[0] battle-idle loop. Nothing is uploaded; the disc never leaves your machine.

Playback carries two retail battle-presentation layers. The character's voice is the XA cue the battle overlay fires as an art starts executing: one channel of XA/XA30.XA per character (Vahn ch0, Noa ch4, Gala ch6 - retail keys the shout on the character, not the individual art), played through the CdlSetfilter path documented in the battle-action reference and demuxed here straight off your disc. The after-image trail is retail's tinted echo - a delayed copy of the animated mesh re-drawn translucently in the character's colour (Vahn red, Noa green, Gala blue), the classic PSX additive semi-transparency trick.
